<p><p>The high-temperature superconductors (HTS) market is categorized into two main types: First Generation HTS (1G) and Second Generation HTS (2G). First Generation HTS, primarily based on materials like niobium-titanium, operate at relatively lower critical temperatures and are mainly used in applications like MRI machines and particle accelerators. Second Generation HTS, utilizing materials like yttrium barium copper oxide, exhibit higher critical temperatures and offer better performance and flexibility, making them suitable for power applications, transmission lines, and advanced electronic devices.</p></p>
