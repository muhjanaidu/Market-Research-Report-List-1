<p><ul><li>Contact-Type</li><li>Noncontact-Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The mechanical temperature sensor market is divided into two main types: contact-type and noncontact-type sensors. Contact-type sensors measure temperature by physical contact with the object, using mechanisms like thermocouples and resistance temperature detectors (RTDs). In contrast, noncontact-type sensors gauge temperature without direct contact, employing technologies such as infrared sensing to detect emitted thermal radiation. Both types serve various applications across industries, with contact sensors offering accuracy for solid surfaces and noncontact sensors ideal for moving or hazardous materials.</p></p>
