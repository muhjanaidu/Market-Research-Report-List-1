<p><p>The non-woven fabrics/textiles market comprises several key types, including spunmelt, wet laid, dry laid, and others. Spunmelt fabrics are produced by combining spunbond and meltblown processes, offering strength and filtration properties. Wet laid fabrics are manufactured using a water-based process, resulting in uniform sheets ideal for medical and hygiene applications. Dry laid fabrics are created from fibers that are spread out and bonded through heat or chemical agents, commonly used in industrial applications. Other types include carded and needle-punched fabrics, addressing diverse industry needs.</p></p>
