<p><p>The smoking cessation drugs market is primarily divided into two categories: nicotine replacement therapies (NRTs) and nicotine-free products. NRTs include options like gums, patches, lozenges, and inhalers that supply controlled doses of nicotine to alleviate withdrawal symptoms and cravings. In contrast, nicotine-free products focus on reducing dependence through non-nicotine medications or natural supplements that manage withdrawal and psychological triggers without delivering nicotine. Both market types aim to support individuals in their journey to quit smoking and improve overall health.</p></p>
