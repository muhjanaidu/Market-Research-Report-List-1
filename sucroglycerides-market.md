<p><p>The sucroglycerides market encompasses various types, primarily monoglycerides and diglycerides. Monoglycerides consist of one fatty acid molecule linked to glycerol, enhancing food texture and acting as emulsifiers. They are widely utilized in baking and dairy products. Diglycerides contain two fatty acid chains and serve similar functions, improving stability and extending shelf life in processed foods. Both types play crucial roles in food formulation, offering benefits like improved moisture retention, flavor enhancement, and consistency in various culinary applications.</p></p>
